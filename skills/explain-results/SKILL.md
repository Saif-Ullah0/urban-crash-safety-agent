# Explain Results Skill

## Purpose
Translate raw risk scores and route data into clear,
actionable plain-English explanations for non-technical users.

## When to Use This Skill
- After getting route or risk scoring results
- User asks "what does this score mean"
- User seems confused by technical output
- Final step before presenting any recommendation

## Execution Steps
1. Take raw tool output (scores, waypoints, risk levels)
2. Identify the most important finding
3. Lead with the bottom line (safe or not safe)
4. Explain the score in relatable terms
5. Give a clear next step or recommendation

## Language Rules
- Never say "0.73 risk score" without explaining what it means
- Always compare to a threshold ("above 0.65 means high risk")
- Use simple words: "risky area" not "elevated collision probability"
- Keep explanations under 3 sentences per waypoint
- Always end with a clear action the user can take

## Example Good Explanation
"The route through Canal Street has a high crash risk score
of 0.71, meaning this area sees significantly more collisions
than average. I recommend confirming before proceeding, or
I can find you an alternative route."
